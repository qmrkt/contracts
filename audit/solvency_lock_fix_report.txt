### VULNERABILITY TITLE:
**Permanent Fund Lock via Invariant-Induced Liveness Failure (Solvency Lock)**

---

### SEVERITY:
**Critical**  
This bug permanently freezes all user funds and contract assets when a market becomes insolvent.

---

### AFFECTED FUNCTION(S):
- `_assert_invariants()` (Lines 646-681)
- `challenge_resolution()` (Line 1219)
- `finalize_resolution()` (Line 1338)
- `cancel()` (Line 1386)

---

### ROOT CAUSE:
The `_assert_invariants()` method incorrectly gates entry into `STATUS_DISPUTED` and `STATUS_CANCELLED` on a strict solvency check:  
`pool_balance >= total_outstanding_cost_basis`.

In this contract, "insolvency" (i.e., `pool_balance < total_outstanding_cost_basis`) is an unavoidable state due to the following reasons:

1. **LMSR Logic**:
   - Traders can legitimately extract more profit than the initial LP cushion if the market moves significantly.

2. **Fee Siphoning**:
   - Every `sell()` operation decrements `pool_balance` by the **gross return** (Line 1063) while diverting fees to separate balances.
   - The initial `bootstrap_deposit` does not account for this drain. High trading volume will inevitably deplete the pool cushion until it falls below the cost basis.

Once this threshold is crossed:
- The contract blocks the **Challenge** and **Cancel** paths.
- If the proposed outcome is also insolvent (`pool_balance < winning_shares`), the **Finalize** path is blocked by `_assert_solvency()`.

This creates a **terminal deadlock** with no exit transition, locking 100% of the funds.

---

### ATTACK PATH:
1. **Create a Market**:
   - Bootstrap the market with an initial deposit.
2. **Drain the Pool Cushion**:
   - Execute high-volume trading (Buy/Sell cycles) until `pool_balance < total_outstanding_cost_basis` (due to fee siphoning and trader profit).
3. **Reach Market Deadline**:
   - A proposer submits an outcome where `total_user_shares[outcome] > pool_balance`.
4. **Trigger Deadlock**:
   - Attempt to **Challenge** → REVERT (`Invariant pool >= tcb fails`).
   - Attempt to **Finalize** → REVERT (`Solvency check pool >= shares fails`).
   - Attempt to **Cancel** → REVERT (`Invariant pool >= tcb fails`).

**Result**: Market is permanently stuck; funds are locked.

---

### IMPACT:
- **All users and LPs are affected**.
- **100% of the market's liquidity is permanently locked** in the contract with no recovery path.

### EXPECTED BEHAVIOR:
The contract must always allow:
- **Challenging a proposal** or
- **Cancelling the market**  
to restore liveness, even if the pool is insolvent.

Invariants should ensure safety (e.g., pro-rata distribution), not block terminal state transitions.

---

### ACTUAL BEHAVIOR:
The contract reverts all attempts to transition the market to a terminal state, locking all funds.

---

### SUGGESTED FIXES:
1. **Relax Solvency Checks**:
   - Allow `challenge_resolution()` and `cancel()` to proceed even if `pool_balance < total_outstanding_cost_basis`.
   - Introduce fallback mechanisms to ensure liveness.

2. **Pro-Rata Distribution**:
   - Implement a pro-rata distribution mechanism for insolvent states to ensure fair recovery of funds.

3. **Fee Accounting Adjustments**:
   - Modify the `sell()` logic to account for fee siphoning and prevent premature depletion of the pool cushion.

4. **Graceful Degradation**:
   - Introduce a "graceful degradation" mode where the contract transitions to a terminal state with partial payouts if solvency cannot be maintained.
